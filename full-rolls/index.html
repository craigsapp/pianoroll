Complete Roll Scans

This page list scans of entire piano rolls in full color at 300 dpi resolution as well as several derivative image formats done with the Stanford Library piano-roll scanner. The 100-dpi JPEG versions of the rolls are 10-15 MB in size and suitable for viewing in a web-browser (click on the image and wait for the full resolution version to be displayed). Full-color uncompressed TIFFs are about 1 GB or larger. The individual compressed color channel files are about 250 MB each, and the 2-color thresholded 300 DPI PNG images are under 1 MB each.

Note that the height of the roll thumbnails indicates the length of the roll (thinner thumbnails are longer rolls). 100-dpi JPEG files are in "visual" orientation, which means that time progresses upwards on most rolls except for Red Welte rolls. In other words, the triangular leader of the roll indicating the start of the roll is placed at the bottom of the image, and bass notes are on the left side of the image. TIFF and PNG images are oriented in "notes" orientation, where the leader is at the top of the image, and bass notes are on the left side of the image.

The green channel of the TIFF images are scanned at 300 DPI, while the red and blue channels are scanned at 150 DPI and then interpolated to 300 DPI by the camera firmware, so in theory the green channel contains the highest resolution image of the rolls.

Test Rolls

These are scans of various test rolls, either hand-made or produced by piano-roll companies, used to calibrate player-piano playback mechanisms.

Calibration Rolls

This section contains scans of a roll used to calibrate the horizontal and vertical DPI at the lower and upper camera setting. The lengthwise DPI was measured to be 300.24±0.21 for the lower camera setting and 300.32±0.19 in the upper camera setting. The camera position should not affect the lengthwise DPI, and it doesn't. The width DPI was measured to be 301.39 pixels/inch for the lower camera setting and 251.48 pixels/inch for the upper camera setting. The caluations for these values are on this spreadsheet.
